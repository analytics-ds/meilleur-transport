## 3008 and 5008, two Peugeot SUVs on the same platform {#context}

The **3008 III** arrived March 2024 with a new coupé design and Stellantis's **STLA Medium** platform. The **5008 III** followed in January 2025 on the same base, but with **249 mm extra wheelbase** and a foldable third row. Both offer the same powertrains: Hybrid 136, 100% electric (e-3008 and e-5008) and Plug-in Hybrid 195 from 2026.

## Five differences that truly separate 3008 and 5008 {#differences}

### 1. Seven seats or not seven seats: the heart of the choice

**The real reason to hesitate.** The 5008 offers a **third row** of two floor-stowed seats, usable by adults up to 1.70 m for short trips, or by two children for all uses. The 3008 is **limited to 5 seats**.

### 2. Nearly identical 5-seat boot, reduced 7-seat boot

In **5-seat** configuration, both boots are close: **520 litres for the 3008**, **348 litres for the 5008** (with row 3 folded). But careful: on the 5008, the third row eats 170 litres under the floor. In **7 seats deployed**, the 5008 only offers **259 litres** behind the rear seats.

### 3. A footprint that changes everything in the city

**4.79 m vs 4.54 m.** 25 cm gap. On a regular supermarket parking, both pass stress-free. In **tight underground parking**, historic **city centres** or a narrow alley in front of your home, the 3008 is much easier to live with.

### 4. Identical hybrid powertrain, slightly different performance

Both share the **1.2 PureTech Hybrid 136** with the e-DCS6 gearbox. The 5008 carries **115 kg more** (1 595 vs 1 480 kg), weighing on performance. **0 to 100 km/h in 10.9 s for the 5008** vs 10.2 s for the 3008.

### 5. Two thousand five hundred euros gap at catalogue

At **GT Hybrid 136** trim, the 3008 is at **39 900 euros**, the 5008 at **42 400 euros**. 2 500 euros gap.

## Which to choose based on your profile {#profile}

### For a family of 4

**The 3008 wins the match.** 520 litres of boot largely enough for holidays as 4, easier footprint daily, 1 600 euros saved over 5 years.

### For a family of 5 or more

**The 5008 becomes the only relevant choice** in the duo. The 3008 is limited to 5 seats, excluded outright.

### For a couple without children

**The 3008** without hesitation. No point paying the 5008 premium and dealing with its 25 extra centimetres for a use that never fills the 2 extra rear seats.

### For regular urban use

**The 3008** wins easily. 4.54 m stays manageable in tight parking and dense traffic. The 5008 demands constant attention.

### For highway + family holidays

**The 5008 takes the edge** if the family counts 5+ or holidays involve bulky gear (bikes, tents).

## 3008 III and 5008 III reliability {#reliability}

Both models are **too recent** (2024 and 2025) to have reliable feedback beyond 100 000 km. Early signals are encouraging: **no major recall**, **few customer complaints** on specialist forums, and a 1.2 hybrid + e-DCS6 thermal design derived from the proven 2023 version.

## Frequently asked questions {#faq}

<details>
<summary>Peugeot 3008 or 5008 for a family of 5?</summary>

The **5008** if the family regularly uses all 5 seats + luggage. The **3008** if it's a family of 5 but with young kids and light luggage, or if the 5th passenger is occasional.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can adults really fit in the 5008's third row?</summary>

For **short trips under an hour**, yes, up to 1.70 m. Beyond, knees touch row 2 backrests and comfort drops fast. For long trips, the 3rd row is for children up to 12.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What's the price gap between 3008 and 5008?</summary>

**2 500 euros at catalogue** between GT Hybrid 136 trims. In real 5-year TCO, the gap drops to **1 580 euros** thanks to better 5008 depreciation recovery.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Do the 3008 and 5008 come in 100% electric?</summary>

Yes. The **e-3008** has been offered since September 2024 with **73 kWh battery and 525 km WLTP range**. The **e-5008** followed in 2025 with the same battery but **502 km range** due to extra weight (~100 kg).
</details>

<details>
<summary>Hybrid or 100% electric, which one to pick?</summary>

**Hybrid** if you do lots of highway and have no home charger. **Electric** if you charge at home and mainly drive in city or commutes under 400 km daily.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I tow a caravan with the 3008 or 5008?</summary>

Yes for both. The 3008 Hybrid 136 is limited to **1 250 kg braked**, the 5008 Hybrid 136 to **1 350 kg braked**. For 1 500+ kg, aim for the upcoming PHEV 195 versions.
</details>

<details>
<summary>3008 or 5008 for 3-year resale?</summary>

The **3008** better preserves absolute value thanks to a wider used market (C-segment in high demand). The **5008** depreciates slightly more in percentage but benefits from rarer supply.
</details>

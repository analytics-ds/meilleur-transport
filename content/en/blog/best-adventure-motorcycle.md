> **At a glance**
> - The **grand tourer adventure** market is dominated by 3 brands: **BMW GS**, **Honda Africa Twin**, **Yamaha Ténéré**, together holding **60% of 2025 European sales**.

**Motorcycle adventure** remains a dream for many riders. The trail, with its **high riding position, long suspension and all-terrain capabilities**, is the ideal bike for eating up miles on asphalt and off. To rank the best [adventure motorcycles](/en/categories/motorcycles/), we kept **10 2026 models** with the criteria that truly matter: range, comfort, load capacity and off-road ability.

### 1. Yamaha Ténéré 700, the segment consensus

The **Ténéré 700** became **the mid-trail reference** since its 2019 launch. Strengths: **204 kg contained weight**, **16-litre tank** (400+ km range), **smooth indestructible CP2 twin**, **long-travel suspension** (215 mm front, 200 mm rear), **21-inch front wheel**.

**Price: 11 199 euros.** A2 version available (47 hp), World Raid version at 13 999 euros with **23-litre tank**.

### 2. Honda Africa Twin CRF1100L, the legend revisited

The **Africa Twin** returned in 2016 after 13 years' absence. **1 084 cc twin**, **102 hp**, **automatic DCT gearbox available** (unique in segment).

![Honda Africa Twin](/images/meilleure-moto-trail/honda-africa-twin.webp "Honda Africa Twin CRF1100L")

**2026 price: 14 799 euros** (standard version), **16 299 euros** with DCT.

### 3. BMW R 1300 GS, the queen of road trails

**THE model that created the segment** and has remained its reference for 40 years. 2024 new generation with **1 300 cc boxer**, **145 hp**, ESA adaptive suspension.

**Base price: 19 500 euros.** With Dynamic Pro + Comfort + Navigation packs, the bill climbs to 24 000 euros.

### 4. KTM 790 Adventure, the sporty that travels

The **KTM 790 Adventure** targets **sporty riders** who want to travel. **799 cc twin**, **95 hp**.

**Price: 12 790 euros.**

The **888 cc Triumph triple** brings a unique character to mid-trails. **108 hp**, **21-inch front wheel**, **long-travel Showa suspension**.

**Price: 14 290 euros** (Rally version specific off-road).

### 6. Ducati DesertX, the pure-race sport

The **Ducati DesertX** is the most off-road-oriented adventure in Ducati range. **937 cc Testastretta twin**, **110 hp**, Öhlins suspension in Rally version.

### 7. Suzuki V-Strom 800DE, the accessible explorer

### 8. KTM 390 Adventure R, the small A2 trail

**177 kg only**, the lightest trail in the ranking. **399 cc single**, 45 hp (A2 direct compliant).

### 9. Moto Guzzi V85 TT, the atypical Italian

The **V85 TT** uses a **unique 853 cc V-twin air-cooled**.

The new generation **Himalayan 450** (2024) finally brings modernity to entry-level adventure. **452 cc single**, **39 hp (A2 direct)**.

## Practical tips for choosing your adventure bike {#tips}

### Mandatory testing before buying

Adventure bikes have **very specific riding positions** (high seat, wide handlebars, forward foot pegs). Schedule **at least 2 dealer test rides**.

### Luggage, a separate investment

For serious travel, count **1 000 to 2 500 extra euros** for aluminium panniers (600 to 1 500 €), 50 L top case (150 to 400 €), waterproof tank bag (80 to 250 €).

### Adventure-specific rider gear

**Off-road motorcycle boot** (shin + reinforced ankle): 250 to 600 €. **Breathable off-road motorcycle pants**: 200 to 500 €.

### Suspension, the area to watch

Adventure motorcycles have **long suspension that wears faster** than a roadster. Plan **fork service/adjustment at 30 000 km** (300 to 600 €).

## Frequently asked questions {#faq}

<details>
<summary>What's the best adventure motorcycle in 2026?</summary>

The **Yamaha Ténéré 700** remains the best value at 11 199 euros. For more road use with more comfort, the **Honda Africa Twin CRF1100L** (14 799 €). For premium touring, the **BMW R 1300 GS** (19 500 €).
</details>

<details>
<summary>Which A2 adventure motorcycle for young rider?</summary>

The **Yamaha Ténéré 700 A2** is the best choice. For tighter budget, the **KTM 390 Adventure R** at 7 090 euros is A2 direct with contained 177 kg weight.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How much insurance for an adventure motorcycle?</summary>

For an experienced rider (5+ years A licence), full coverage with luggage covered:
- **Ténéré 700**: 850 to 1 200 €/year
- **Africa Twin**: 950 to 1 300 €/year
- **BMW R 1300 GS**: 1 100 to 1 500 €/year
</details>

<details>
<summary>Which adventure motorcycle for a world tour?</summary>

The **Yamaha Ténéré 700** is the reference for its mechanical simplicity, contained weight and global parts network. The **Royal Enfield Himalayan 450** is the economic alternative.
</details>

<details>
<summary>700 cc or 1 200 cc adventure, which to pick?</summary>

**700 cc** if you travel mainly solo, on mixed road-trail, prioritising agility. **1 200 cc** if two-up with luggage, on long highway trips.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What range for travel?</summary>

Adventure motorcycles offer **350 to 500 km range** depending on tank:
- **Ténéré 700**: 16 L, ~400 km
- **Ténéré 700 World Raid**: 23 L, ~550 km
- **Africa Twin**: 18.8 L, ~430 km
- **BMW GS Adventure**: 30 L, ~700 km
</details>

<details>
<summary>What's the difference between trail and adventure?</summary>

**Trail**: generic French term for all high-riding mixed-wheel bikes. **Adventure**: Anglo-Saxon term specifically for **big road tourer trails** (BMW GS, Africa Twin). In 2026, terms have become interchangeable.
</details>

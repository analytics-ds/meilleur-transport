> **At a glance**
> - An A2 motorcycle is limited to **35 kW (47.5 hp)** by European law, with a **maximum power-to-weight ratio of 0.2 kW/kg**.
> - The **best-seller remains the Yamaha MT-07** at 8 699 euros, ideal for a first purchase.
> - **Factory-restricted bikes** (MT-07, CB500F, Z650) cost on average **800 to 1 500 euros less** than bikes needing an external restriction kit.

The A2 licence concerns **over 80 000 French riders** each year who start motorcycling after the test. For 2 mandatory years, you can only ride bikes limited to 47.5 hp. To sort through the wide offer, we ranked the **10 best [A2 motorcycles](/en/categories/motorcycles/)** in 2026, with the objective criteria that matter: price, weight, seat height, versatility and value.

## How to choose your A2 motorcycle {#choosing}

### Weight and seat height

For a **first purchase**, prefer a bike under 190 kg with an accessible seat (both feet flat).

### Factory restriction vs external kit

**Factory restriction**: MT-07, Z650, SV650, Ténéré 700. Simpler, official certification, easy unrestriction. **A2 direct (no restriction)**: CB500F, NC750X, KTM 390.

### 5-year versatility

If you want to **keep the bike long-term**, pick a model that stays interesting once unrestricted. MT-07 at full power (73 hp) stays excellent, CB500F at native version stays limited to 47 hp even after 2 years.

### Insurance

Expect:
- **A2-restricted 500-650 cc**: 600 to 800 €/year full cover for young rider
- **A2-restricted 750+ cc**: 700 to 1 000 €/year full cover

### Maintenance cost

Single (KTM 390, Himalayan, G 310 R): cheapest, **200 to 300 €/year**. Twin (MT-07, Z650, CB500F): **350 to 500 €/year**. Triple or 4-cylinder: **500 to 700 €/year**.

## Practical tips for first purchase {#tips}

### Avoid classic young rider pitfalls

- **Don't aim too big or too powerful** even restricted
- **Prefer new or recent used** (under 3 years, under 20 000 km)
- **Mandatory dealer test ride**

### Rider gear, a budget to plan

For **safe riding**, count **1 000 to 1 500 extra euros** for complete gear.

## Frequently asked questions {#faq}

<details>
<summary>What's the best A2 motorcycle to start?</summary>

The **Yamaha MT-07** remains the best versatile choice: accessible ergonomics, smooth engine, simple factory restriction. For tight budget, the **Honda CB500F** at 7 199 euros is an excellent alternative.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I ride an A2 motorcycle with B licence + 125?</summary>

No. The B licence + 7-hour training allows riding a **maximum 125 cc** (15 hp). For an A2 bike (47 hp, 500-750 cc), the **A2 licence** is mandatory via exam.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How much does motorcycle restriction cost?</summary>

Factory restriction is **included in the new bike price** (MT-07, Z650, Ténéré 700). **Unrestricting after 2 years** costs **250 to 400 euros** parts + labour.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Which A2 motorcycle for travel?</summary>

**The Yamaha Ténéré 700 A2** is the trail/travel reference in A2. Alternative lower seat: the **Honda NC750X** in A2 version.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How much insurance for an A2 motorcycle?</summary>

For a **young A2 rider 18-25**, third-party + theft + damage: **600 to 900 euros per year** on an MT-07 or Z650.
</details>

<details>
<summary>MT-07 or MT-09 in A2?</summary>

**MT-07 A2** stays more relevant. See our detailed [MT-07 vs MT-09](/en/blog/yamaha-mt07-vs-mt09/) comparison: MT-09 restricted loses 60% of its power.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How long do you stay in A2?</summary>

**2 years minimum** from A2 licence date. After this period, follow a **7-hour training** to upgrade to A licence without exam.
</details>

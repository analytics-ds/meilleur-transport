### 1. Dacia Jogger Hybrid 140, the unbeatable value

The **Dacia Jogger Hybrid** transforms the 7-seater segment since 2023. **Full hybrid** with the Renault E-Tech 140 engine, **mixed WLTP consumption at 4.8 l/100 km**, **starting price 22 450 euros**. The **cheapest 7-seater on the market**, new or used.

**Limits**: rear space adapted to children rather than adults, premium equipment absent. But for the price, nobody does better.

### 2. Peugeot 5008 Hybrid 136, the tech compromise

The **new Peugeot 5008 III** launched in 2025 offers **48V mild hybrid** saving around 15% fuel in mixed use.

**GT Hybrid 136 price: 38 500 euros.** See our [Peugeot 3008 vs 5008](/en/blog/peugeot-3008-vs-5008/) comparison.

### 3. Toyota Prius+ (used only)

No longer sold new since 2021, the **Toyota Prius+** remains an excellent used buy. **Toyota full hybrid** (reliability reference), **record 4.9 l/100 km consumption**. In 2026, 2017-2020 models trade between **12 000 and 18 000 euros**.

**Major asset**: Toyota reliability. Some units exceed 300 000 km without major hybrid system intervention.

### 4. Kia Sorento HEV, the balanced Korean SUV

The **Kia Sorento HEV** in full hybrid offers an interesting compromise: **230 combined hp**, **8 seats (family pack)** or **7 seats standard**.

**Price: 48 990 euros.** **7-year manufacturer warranty**.

### 5. Kia Sorento PHEV, the plug-in hybrid

**Price: 54 990 euros.** Only profitable if plugging in daily.

### 6. Hyundai Santa Fe HEV, the Sorento's cousin

**Same Hyundai-Kia group**, same architecture, different design and more **tech and leather** finishes.

## How to choose your hybrid 7-seater car {#choosing}

### Use of seats 6 and 7

**Regular adult use of seats 6 and 7**: Toyota Highlander, Kia Sorento, Hyundai Santa Fe. **Children or occasional use**: Peugeot 5008, Dacia Jogger. **Avoid if often 7 adults**: Mercedes GLB.

### Hybrid type

- **Mild hybrid 48V**: 10-15% fuel saving, no pure electric mode
- **Full hybrid (HEV)**: 20-30% saving, electric mode at low speed
- **Plug-in hybrid (PHEV)**: 50-80 km electric + full hybrid after. **Only interesting with home charging**.

### Total budget

- **Under 25 000 euros**: Dacia Jogger Hybrid (only choice)
- **30 000 to 45 000 euros**: Peugeot 5008 Hybrid, Toyota Prius+ recent used
- **45 000 to 60 000 euros**: Kia Sorento, Hyundai Santa Fe, Toyota Highlander, Mercedes GLB

## Practical buying tips {#tips}

### Test third-row access with your children

**Seats 6 and 7** access by folding the 2nd row bench. The manoeuvre must be **simple for a 6-year-old**. Test in dealership with your actual children.

### Anticipate the "real" 7-seat boot

Forgotten number: **with all 7 seats deployed**, the boot drops to **212 L on the Jogger**, **259 L on the 5008**, **187 L on the Sorento HEV**. **Insufficient for holiday trips for 7**. Plan a **roof rack** or **roof box** for long trips.

### Plug-in hybrid only with home outlet

**Simple rule**: don't buy a plug-in hybrid if you have no home or work charging. Without regular charging, the car runs in classic hybrid but with **150 extra kg of battery** in the boot, **degrading consumption by 10-20%** vs HEV.

### Recent used, the good deal

The hybrid 7-seater used market is active. On a **Kia Sorento HEV 2022 with 50 000 km**, count **30 000 to 34 000 euros**, i.e. **15 000 euros less** than equivalent new. See our [used car buying guide](/en/blog/buying-used-car-guide/).

## Frequently asked questions {#faq}

<details>
<summary>Cheapest hybrid 7-seater in 2026?</summary>

The **Dacia Jogger Hybrid 140** at **22 450 euros** in Expression trim. The only new 7-seater hybrid available under 30 000 euros in France.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can adults fit in seats 6 and 7?</summary>

Yes for premium SUVs (**Kia Sorento**, **Hyundai Santa Fe**, **Toyota Highlander**) thanks to their 2.8 m+ wheelbase. For more compact family cars, seats 6 and 7 suit children up to 12 or short-trip small adults.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What real consumption for a hybrid 7-seater?</summary>

Real mixed use per 15 000 km/year:
- **Dacia Jogger Hybrid**: 4.8 to 5.5 l/100 km
- **Peugeot 5008 Hybrid 136**: 5.5 to 6.2 l/100 km
- **Toyota Prius+**: 4.9 to 5.5 l/100 km
- **Kia Sorento HEV**: 6.5 to 7.5 l/100 km
- **Toyota Highlander**: 7.1 to 7.8 l/100 km
</details>

<details>
<summary>Simple or plug-in hybrid for 7-seater?</summary>

**Simple hybrid (HEV)** for most uses: more reliable, cheaper, no home outlet needed. **Plug-in hybrid (PHEV)** only if you can **charge daily** and trips stay under 60 km.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What reliability for a hybrid 7-seater?</summary>

**Toyota and Hyundai-Kia** dominate hybrid reliability with 10+ years of hindsight. **Peugeot and Dacia** use Stellantis and Renault E-Tech technologies, mature since 2020 but with less hindsight in 7-seat version.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I tow a caravan with a hybrid 7-seater?</summary>

Yes, but with variable capacities:
- **Dacia Jogger Hybrid**: 750 kg (insufficient for real caravans)
- **Peugeot 5008 Hybrid**: 1 350 kg
- **Kia Sorento HEV**: 1 650 kg
- **Toyota Highlander**: 2 000 kg
</details>

<details>
<summary>SUV or MPV for hybrid 7-seater?</summary>

The **7-seater SUV** dominates the 2026 market. Real **MPVs** have nearly disappeared. The **Dacia Jogger** stays the only compact accessible 7-seater estate on the market.
</details>

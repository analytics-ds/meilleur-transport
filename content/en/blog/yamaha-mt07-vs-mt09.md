## MT-07 and MT-09, two cousin Yamaha roadsters {#context}

Yamaha built its whole MT lineup around these two models. The **MT-07** launched in 2014 was designed as an **accessible bike** with its smooth, playful CP2 twin. The **MT-09** from 2013 introduced the **CP3 triple** that became Yamaha's signature sound.

## Five differences that truly separate MT-07 and MT-09 {#differences}

### 1. Forty-six horsepower gap between the two roadsters

**73 hp for the MT-07**, **119 hp for the MT-09**. On the road, the gap shows on every throttle open. Restricted to A2 (35 kW / 47 hp), **both bikes become very close** in performance.

### 2. The MT-09 is an electronic bike, the MT-07 stays mechanical

The MT-09 packs in 2026: **3-level traction control**, **cornering ABS**, **4 riding modes** (Sport, Street, Rain, Custom), **standard bidirectional quickshifter**, **5" TFT colour display** with smartphone connectivity. The MT-07 stays **purely mechanical**.

### 3. A lighter, more agile, more accessible MT-07

**184 kg wet** for the MT-07, vs **193 kg** for the MT-09. 9 kg gap, doesn't seem much, but **positioned well** (MT-07 has lower centre of gravity), it changes urban agility. Seat at **805 mm** on MT-07 vs **825 mm** on MT-09.

### 4. A cheaper MT-07 upfront but higher-maintenance MT-09

**New price**: 8 699 euros for the MT-07, 11 199 euros for the MT-09. **2 500 euros gap** at start. Yearly maintenance: **380 euros/year for MT-07** vs **520 euros/year for MT-09**.

### 5. A more pleasure-oriented MT-09, a more versatile MT-07

The MT-07 accepts **all uses**: city, home-work, rides, two-up travel with side bags. The MT-09 is **mainly oriented toward pleasure on winding roads**.

## Which to choose based on your profile {#profile}

### For a young A2 licence

**MT-07 without hesitation.** Lighter, lower seat, gentler throttle, simple A2 restriction (35 kW).

### For an experienced rider seeking thrills

**MT-09 without debate.** 119 hp, electronic controls, quickshifter, 3 cylinders with incredible sound.

### For mainly urban use

**MT-07.** Easier to slip between cars, more agile on slippery surfaces, lower urban consumption.

### For long trips

**MT-09** thanks to its **93 Nm of torque** allowing **130 km/h highway pace without straining the engine**.

### For occasional track outings

**MT-09** clearly. Electronics, KYB suspension, Brembo brakes and quickshifter make it an **ideal amateur circuit bike**.

### For two-up riding

**MT-07** for its **smoother ergonomics**, more reassuring for a passenger.

## MT-07 and MT-09 reliability {#reliability}

### MT-07: the nearly indestructible CP2 twin

The **CP2 689 cc** is known for its exceptional robustness: over **100 000 km without major intervention** isn't rare on 2014-2018 models still on the road.

### MT-09: a slightly more sensitive but reliable triple

The **CP3 890 cc** 2024 version is newer and integrates lessons from recent years. 2014-2020 MT-09s had some **rough engine mapping** issues fixed by 2021 and 2024 versions.

## Frequently asked questions {#faq}

<details>
<summary>MT-07 or MT-09 restricted A2, which one to choose?</summary>

The **MT-07 A2** stays more relevant. The 689 cc twin keeps **85% of its character** at 35 kW thanks to engine smoothness. The MT-09 A2 loses **60% of its power** and all the triple's personality.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What sensation difference between 2 and 3 cylinders?</summary>

The **CP2** twin offers a smooth, linear, predictable character with a raw offbeat sound. The **CP3** triple has sharper rev climb, more aggressive tone with unique acceleration sound.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How much insurance on these two bikes?</summary>

For a **30-year-old rider with 3 years A licence**, third-party + theft + damage: **600 to 800 €/year for MT-07** and **750 to 1 000 €/year for MT-09**.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I easily unrestrict an A2 MT-07 or MT-09?</summary>

**Yes for both**, after the 2 mandatory A2 years. Unrestriction replaces a **motor kit (intake throttles) + ECU mapping**. Yamaha cost: **150 to 250 € parts**, **1 to 2 hours labour**.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Which MT for a tall rider (1.90 m)?</summary>

**MT-09** more suited. Its **825 mm seat** and stretched ergonomics fit tall riders better.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Which one lasts longer?</summary>

**Technical tie**: both Yamaha engines are among the market's most reliable. Beyond that, the **CP2** of MT-07 has slightly superior longevity thanks to its mechanical simplicity.
</details>

<details>
<summary>MT-07 or MT-09 for touring?</summary>

The **MT-09** for highway comfort (torque, suspension). But the **MT-07** stays more versatile for mixed road + unpaved trail touring.
</details>

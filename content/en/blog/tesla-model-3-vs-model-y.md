## Model 3 and Model Y, two Teslas on the same platform {#context}

Since the **Highland** facelift in 2024 for the Model 3 and **Juniper** in 2025 for the Model Y, Tesla shares the same technical base on its two mid-range models. **Same battery** (79 kWh in Long Range), same rear powertrain, same V4 Supercharge, same Autopilot. The difference plays out on the body: **three-box sedan for the Model 3**, **SUV with tailgate for the Model Y**.

### Gaps that widen in daily use

On paper, 4 000 euros seem hard to justify. In real life, **the tailgate changes everything**. Loading a bike, an XL pram or IKEA furniture into a Model 3 requires gymnastics. In a Model Y, it rolls in naturally. Conversely, **pulling a Model Y out of an underground parking** with 1.62 m height sometimes demands lowering the height barriers (1.80 m required), while the Model 3 clears all standard European heights.

## Five differences that truly separate Model 3 and Model Y {#differences}

### 1. A tailgate boot that changes daily life

**Model Y**: tailgate opening, 740 mm loading threshold, square 822-litre volume. **Model 3**: sedan trunk opening, higher 800 mm threshold, elongated 594-litre volume.

### 2. Fifty extra kilometres of real range for the Model 3

The Model 3 consumes **13.2 kWh/100 km WLTP** vs 14.9 for the Model Y. On the **same 79 kWh battery**, that gives **629 km WLTP for the Model 3** vs 586 km for the Model Y. In real life, around **50 km of real-world range** difference.

### 3. A taller, heavier, more comfortable Model Y

**167 kg more for the Model Y**, 18 cm more height. Direct consequences: elevated driving position, **vastly better visibility**, easier access for children and seniors. Flip side: slightly softer dynamics, more pronounced body roll in corners, higher consumption.

### 4. The Model Y Performance crushes the Model 3 Performance

Tesla chose to **reserve true performance to the Model Y Performance** in 2026. With **514 hp** (vs 381 hp for Model 3 Performance), the Model Y Performance does 0-100 km/h in **3.5 seconds** vs 4.4 s for the Model 3 Performance.

### 5. Four thousand euros gap at equivalent trim

In Long Range configuration, the Model 3 starts at **44 990 euros** and the Model Y at **48 990 euros** (Tesla France pricing March 2026). Before signing, our [used car buying guide](/en/blog/buying-used-car-guide/) highlights the Tesla-specific checks.

## Which to choose based on your profile {#profile}

### For a family with children

**Model Y without hesitation.** Easier rear access thanks to height, doors that open 90° to simplify child seat installation, practical tailgate boot with a pram.

### For a couple without children

**Model 3 wins.** More dynamic at the wheel, more efficient, and the 4 000 euros saved can fund a home charger or 2 years of insurance.

### For a highway driver

**Close match but the Model 3 takes the lead.** Superior range (50 extra km in real life) and 10% lower consumption pay off on every long trip. On a Paris-Marseille (775 km), the Model 3 needs 2 Supercharge stops vs 3 for the Model Y.

### On a tight budget

**Model 3 Propulsion at 39 990 euros** stays the most accessible Tesla entry point. With the 4 000 € bonus, the bill drops to **35 990 euros**.

### For mixed pro-personal use

A self-employed professional will lean toward the **Model Y**. Cargo capacity and 1 600 kg towing capacity avoid buying a separate small van. For more analysis on professional needs, see our [professional freight transport](/en/blog/professional-freight-transport/) dossier.

## Model 3 and Model Y reliability {#reliability}

Tesla Model 3 and Model Y built in **Shanghai** (the factory supplying Europe) show steadily improving reliability scores since 2022. Early year finish issues (door seals, paint) have been corrected.

**The battery** stays very reliable: around 10-12% degradation over 200 000 km on average per Teslascope data. Battery warranty is **8 years or 192 000 km** with a minimum 70% capacity guarantee.

## Frequently asked questions {#faq}

<details>
<summary>Is the Model Y Juniper really different from the old Model Y?</summary>

Yes. The **Juniper** facelift in 2025 brings a sharper front end, a new dashboard inspired by the Model 3 Highland, the addition of a **rear passenger screen**, reworked insulation and adaptive suspension as standard. Range gains around 40 km WLTP.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What's the real price gap between Model 3 and Model Y?</summary>

In equivalent Long Range trim, **4 000 euros at catalogue** (44 990 vs 48 990 euros in March 2026). In 5-year TCO, the gap drops to around **2 450 euros** thanks to better Model Y residuals.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I plug a Model 3 into a non-Tesla charger?</summary>

Yes, no problem. The Model 3 (like the Model Y) uses the **CCS Combo connector** standard in Europe since 2020. All public chargers (Ionity, Fastned, Allego, Total Energies, etc.) are compatible.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Which Tesla for mainly urban use?</summary>

The **Model 3**. More compact footprint (1.44 m height vs 1.62 m), better aerodynamics reducing urban consumption, superior rear visibility thanks to slimmer pillars.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Model 3 or Model Y for resale?</summary>

The **Model Y** holds its value one notch above the Model 3 in the French used market. 3-year Argus rating: 62% for the Model Y vs 58% for the Model 3 in Long Range trim.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I tow a caravan with a Tesla?</summary>

Yes with the Model Y (**1 600 kg braked capacity**) which covers most European caravans. The Model 3 is limited to **1 000 kg braked**, reserving it for light trailers and small caravans.
</details>
